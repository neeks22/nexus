# 1. SOCIAL MEDIA CALENDAR (90 DAYS)

## Platform Posting Schedule (Daily Rhythm)

| Platform     | Frequency          | Best Times (Ottawa ET)         | Content Type                        |
|--------------|--------------------|-------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| Facebook     | 2x/day             | 7:00 AM, 5:30 PM             | Long-form, stories, shares, lives   |
| Instagram    | 1 Feed + 2 Stories/day, 3 Reels/week | Feed 12:00 PM, Stories 9 AM & 7 PM, Reels 6 PM | Visual-first, carousels, Reels |
| TikTok       | 3x/week            | Tue/Thu/Sat 6:00 PM           | Short-form vertical video            |
| YouTube      | 2x/month           | Saturday 10:00 AM             | Long-form educational/walkthrough    |

**Bilingual Rule:** Post in English. Add a French line or do a separate French version for 1 in every 3 posts. For TikTok/Reels, add French captions. Tag #Ottawa #Gatineau on every post to capture both sides of the river.

## MONTH 1: AWARENESS (Weeks 1-4)
*Theme: "Who are we, what do we do, and why should you trust us?"*

### WEEK 1: Grand Introduction

**Monday**
- **Facebook AM (7:00):** "Meet the Team" -- Group photo of all staff in front of the lot. Caption: "We're [DEALERSHIP NAME] and we believe everyone deserves a reliable ride. We specialize in helping people who've been turned away by other dealers. Bad credit? New to Canada? We've helped 500+ families get on the road. Here's how we're different..." Include 3 bullet points about your process. End with "DM us or call [PHONE] -- no judgment, just solutions."
- **Facebook PM (5:30):** Share a local Ottawa news article about rising car prices. Add your take: "This is exactly why we work so hard to keep our payments affordable. Most of our customers pay between $250-$450/month."
- **Instagram Feed (12:00):** Same team photo, cropped square. Caption shorter: "Bad credit? New to Canada? We've helped 500+ Ottawa families get approved. No judgment. Just keys. Link in bio." Use carousel: slide 1 = team photo, slide 2 = "What we do" text graphic, slide 3 = "How it works" 3 steps.
- **Instagram Stories (9 AM):** Poll -- "Have you ever been denied at a car dealership? Yes / No"
- **Instagram Stories (7 PM):** Behind-the-scenes: quick video walking through the lot saying "Good evening Ottawa! Here's what just came in today..."

**Tuesday**
- **Facebook AM:** "Why We Exist" story post. The owner's personal story: "I started [DEALERSHIP NAME] because I watched my [family member/friend] get humiliated at a dealership. They had bad credit and were treated like they didn't matter. That's not how we do things here."
- **Facebook PM:** New arrival post -- specific vehicle. "JUST IN: 2021 Hyundai Tucson AWD, 68,000 km, heated seats, backup camera. Perfect for Ottawa winters. Payments starting at $[XX]/bi-weekly. Credit issues? We work with 15+ lenders to find you the best rate."
- **Instagram Feed:** Same Tucson, 3-angle carousel. Short punchy caption.
- **Instagram Stories AM:** "Quick question" sticker -- "What's your dream car?"
- **Instagram Stories PM:** Boomerang of keys being tossed, text overlay "Someone's getting approved today"
- **TikTok (6 PM):** Hook: "POV: You've been rejected at 3 dealerships and you walk into ours." Show the warm greeting, the simple application, the handshake, the key handoff. Trending sound. 15 seconds.

**Wednesday**
- **Facebook AM:** Educational post -- "5 Things That ACTUALLY Hurt Your Credit Score (And 3 That Don't)" -- Listicle format. Position as helpful expert.
- **Facebook PM:** Customer spotlight (with permission) -- "Congrats to Sarah! She came to us after being turned down twice. Today she drove home in a 2020 RAV4. Welcome to the family!" Photo of customer with car.
- **Instagram Feed:** Same customer delivery photo with confetti emoji-free caption. "Another family on the road. Sarah came to us after 2 rejections. Same-day approval. That's what we do."
- **Instagram Stories AM:** Share the educational post from Facebook with "Swipe up to read"
- **Instagram Stories PM:** Video testimonial clip from Sarah (even 10 seconds is enough)

**Thursday**
- **Facebook AM:** "Myth Buster Monday... on a Thursday" -- "MYTH: You need 20% down to get a car with bad credit. TRUTH: We've approved customers with $0 down. It depends on your full financial picture, not just one number." Include a simple graphic.
- **Facebook PM:** Inventory showcase -- "Weekend Ready" -- 3 vehicles in a collage. "Heading into the weekend and these 3 just hit the lot. Which one's calling your name? 1, 2, or 3?"
- **Instagram Feed:** Same 3-car carousel. "Comment 1, 2, or 3 -- which one are you taking home this weekend?"
- **Instagram Stories AM:** Countdown sticker -- "Weekend deals drop in 2 days"
- **Instagram Stories PM:** Quick walkaround video of best vehicle of the 3.

**Friday**
- **Facebook AM:** "Finance Friday" -- "Here's exactly what happens when you apply with us: Step 1: Fill out our 60-second app (link). Step 2: We match you with the best lender from our 15+ partners. Step 3: You pick your car. Step 4: You drive home. Average approval time: 2 hours." Include a simple infographic.
- **Facebook PM:** Weekend push -- "We're open Saturday 9-5! Walk in or book your appointment. This weekend's special: [VEHICLE] at $[XX]/bi-weekly with $0 down OAC."
- **Instagram Feed:** Reel -- "How we approve people other dealers can't" -- 30 sec, show the process, upbeat music.
- **Instagram Stories AM:** Share the Finance Friday post
- **Instagram Stories PM:** "Weekend hours" graphic with address and phone number
- **TikTok (6 PM):** "Things that WON'T stop you from getting approved at our dealership" -- list format with text on screen: bankruptcy, consumer proposal, new to Canada, no credit history, missed payments. Trending sound. Each item appears on screen with a "nope, still approved" gesture.

**Saturday**
- **Facebook AM:** "Saturday Morning Lot Tour" -- Facebook Live or pre-recorded 2-3 min video walking the lot showing 5-6 vehicles with prices. Casual, conversational tone.
- **Facebook PM:** Share any customer delivery from the day in real-time.
- **Instagram Feed:** Best photo from any Saturday delivery. If no delivery, post a "weekend vibes" shot of the clean lot.
- **Instagram Stories:** Throughout the day -- document deliveries, test drives, busy lot activity. 3-5 story posts.
- **TikTok (6 PM):** "Saturday at a car dealership that actually cares" -- day-in-the-life format. 15-30 sec montage.

**Sunday**
- **Facebook PM (5:30 only):** Inspirational/motivational post -- "A year ago, [CUSTOMER] had a 580 credit score and no car. Today they've made 12 on-time payments and their score is 670. A car isn't just transportation -- it's a tool to rebuild your life."
- **Instagram Stories:** 1 story -- "We're back tomorrow at 9 AM. What are you looking for?" with question sticker.

## How to Film a Walkaround in 3 Minutes

### Setup (30 seconds)
1. Clean the car (or at minimum, make sure it's not visibly dirty)
2. Park it in a good spot -- avoid clutter in the background. Face it so the sun is behind you (not behind the car)
3. Mount your phone on the tripod OR have someone hold it. Portrait for TikTok/Reels, landscape for YouTube/Facebook
4. Clip on your lav mic
5. Open your camera app. Set to video. Set to 1080p (or 4K if your phone handles it)

### Filming (2 minutes)
**Shot 1 -- The Approach (5 sec):** Start 10 feet away. Walk toward the front of the car. Say: "Hey, check out this [YEAR MAKE MODEL] that just hit our lot."

**Shot 2 -- Front Quarter (10 sec):** Stand at the front driver's side corner. Show the front end. Mention the color, trim, and a visual feature (LED headlights, grille design).

**Shot 3 -- Driver Side Walk (10 sec):** Walk slowly along the driver's side. Mention drivetrain (AWD) and something about the condition ("not a scratch on it").

**Shot 4 -- Rear (5 sec):** Quick shot of the back. Mention cargo space if it's an SUV. "Plenty of room for groceries, strollers, hockey bags."

**Shot 5 -- Interior (15 sec):** Open the driver's door. Show the dashboard, infotainment screen, seats. Sit in the driver's seat. Mention 2-3 features: heated seats, backup camera, CarPlay.

**Shot 6 -- The Close (10 sec):** Get out, stand by the car, face the camera. "$[AMOUNT] bi-weekly, all credit welcome. DM us or call [PHONE]. This one won't last."

### Post-Filming (30 seconds)
1. Watch the video once to make sure audio is clear
2. If anything is muffled or there's wind noise, re-film the affected shot
3. Transfer to CapCut for editing (see editing section below)

## How to Film a Customer Testimonial in 2 Minutes

### Before You Film
- Ask permission: "Would you mind sharing a quick 30-second video about your experience? It helps other people like you know we're legit."
- Most people say yes when they're happy. Ask right after key handoff when emotions are high.
- If they're nervous: "I'll ask you 2 questions, just answer naturally. We can delete it if you don't like it."

### Setup (20 seconds)
1. Stand them next to their new car (this is the money shot)
2. Phone on tripod or have someone hold it, at chest height
3. Portrait mode for Reels/TikTok, landscape for Facebook/YouTube
4. Make sure the car and the customer's face are both visible
5. Lav mic on the customer

### The 2 Questions (60-90 seconds of filming)
**Question 1:** "Tell us a bit about your situation before you came here." (Let them talk for 20-30 seconds. They'll naturally share their struggle.)

**Question 2:** "And how was the experience with us?" (They'll naturally share the positive outcome. Let them talk for 20-30 seconds.)

**Question 3 (optional):** "What would you say to someone who's in the same situation you were in?" (This creates the most shareable content.)

### Close
Film them waving, giving a thumbs up, or standing proudly next to their car for 5 seconds. This becomes your B-roll.

## How to Edit in CapCut in 5 Minutes

**Download:** CapCut (free, iOS and Android). Use the free version -- you don't need Pro.

### Step-by-Step Editing Workflow

**Step 1: Import (15 sec)**
- Open CapCut, tap "New Project"
- Select your video clips from camera roll
- They'll auto-arrange on the timeline

**Step 2: Trim the Fat (90 sec)**
- Play through the video
- Tap on any clip, drag the edges to trim out pauses, "umms," and dead air
- Split clips: position the playhead, tap "Split" to remove a section
- Rule: If it's boring, cut it. Keep only the good parts.

**Step 3: Add Text (60 sec)**
- Tap "Text" then "Add Text"
- Add the vehicle name, price, and your dealership name
- Position text at the top or bottom (avoid center -- that's where the person is)
- Font: Bold, white text with black outline or background box. Easy to read.
- For TikTok/Reels: Add auto-captions. Tap "Text" > "Auto Captions" > select English or French

**Step 4: Add Music (30 sec)**
- Tap "Audio" > "Sounds"
- Search for trending sounds (CapCut shows what's popular)
- Or use a simple upbeat background track
- Lower the music volume to 20-30% so the voice is clear
- For TikTok: Add the trending sound IN TikTok, not in CapCut (better algorithm)

**Step 5: Export (15 sec)**
- Tap "Export" (top right)
- Resolution: 1080p
- Frame rate: 30fps
- Save to camera roll
- DONE

### CapCut Pro Tips
- Use "Auto Reframe" to convert landscape video to portrait (or vice versa)
- Use "Remove Background" on clips where you want text behind the person
- Use "Speed" to create slow-mo moments (key handoff, dramatic reveal)
- Use CapCut templates: search "car dealership" for pre-built templates you can drop your clips into

## Lighting and Audio Tips for a Car Lot

### Lighting
- **Best time to film:** "Golden hour" -- the hour after sunrise or the hour before sunset. The light is warm and even. Cars look amazing.
- **Midday (10 AM - 2 PM):** Harsh shadows. Film in shaded areas or with the sun behind you (not behind the car). If you must film midday, cloudy days are your best friend.
- **Overcast days:** Actually great for filming. Soft, even light. No harsh shadows on the car or on faces.
- **Indoor filming:** Use your small LED panel. Position it at 45 degrees to the person's face, slightly above eye level. Avoid overhead fluorescent-only lighting.
- **At night:** Avoid. Cars look bad under lot lights. If you must, park the car under the brightest light and use your LED panel for the person.

### Audio
- **Always use the lav mic for talking-head content.** Phone mics pick up wind, traffic, and lot noise.
- **Wind:** If it's windy, use the foam cover on your lav mic. If you don't have one, film inside the car with windows up.
- **Traffic noise:** Film during quieter hours if possible. Early morning or after 6 PM on weekdays. If there's background noise, the lav mic will still isolate the voice reasonably well.
- **Car noise on the lot:** If cars are running nearby, wait for them to turn off or move to a quieter corner of the lot.
- **Test audio BEFORE filming the whole thing.** Film a 5-second test, play it back. If you can hear the voice clearly, you're good.
