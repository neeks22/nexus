## 2. Cold Lead Warming: Current Process and Pain Points

### The Typical Dealership Process (What Exists Today)

1. Web lead comes in via VDP form, chat, or phone call
2. CRM assigns lead to a sales rep or BDC agent
3. First contact attempt (usually phone call or templated email)
4. If no response: 1-2 more follow-up attempts over a few days
5. Lead marked "cold" or "lost" in CRM
6. Maybe a drip email campaign runs, maybe not
7. Lead effectively dies in the database

### What Sucks About It

- **Insufficient follow-up frequency:** Most dealerships follow up 1-2 times, then stop. Studies show it takes 5-8 touches to convert a lead.
- **Generic, impersonal messaging:** "If messages feel automated, generic, or too pushy, trust breaks down quickly." Leads go cold when follow-ups do not feel relevant.
- **No behavioral triggers:** Dealerships rarely monitor engagement signals (email opens, link clicks, website returns) to time outreach.
- **Timing misalignment:** Leads may not be ready to buy when they first submit a form. No mechanism to re-engage when timing improves.
- **Channel monotony:** Over-reliance on phone calls or email alone. Effective re-engagement requires multi-channel (SMS, email, phone, social).
- **No vehicle-specific context:** Follow-ups rarely reference the specific vehicle the customer looked at. A message like "Still looking at that Silverado? We have two in stock" dramatically outperforms generic templates.
- **Database rot:** 89% of dealers do not include alternative available vehicles in their responses. Leads with interest in sold inventory get zero follow-up on similar units.

### What Best Practice Looks Like

- Contextual automation referencing specific vehicles and browsing history
- Behavior-based triggers (re-engage when a "dead" lead revisits the website)
- Multi-channel cadence: text + email + phone across 5-8 touches
- Reactivation campaigns aligned to buying cycle (~30-90 day intervals)
- Automated re-engagement when price drops or new matching inventory arrives

## 5. Critical Integrations

### Must-Have (Dealership Will Not Buy Without These)

1. **DMS (Dealer Management System)** -- The backbone. CDK Global, Reynolds & Reynolds, Tekion, PBS Systems (Canadian), Dealertrack (Cox). Every customer record, deal jacket, and accounting entry flows through DMS.

2. **CRM** -- Where leads live. DealerSocket, Elead, VinSolutions, DriveCentric, Activix (Canada). Your AI must read and write to their CRM or it is useless.

3. **Inventory Management** -- Real-time vehicle availability is essential for AI responses. Must sync with vAuto, Dealerslink, HomeNet, or the DMS inventory feed. Without this, AI responses reference vehicles that are already sold.

4. **OEM Portals** -- Manufacturer-specific lead programs (e.g., GM iMR, Ford Smart VINCENT, Toyota Engage). OEM leads flow through these portals and dealers must respond within OEM-mandated timeframes or lose co-op dollars.

### Should-Have (Major Value-Add)

5. **Website / Digital Retailing Platform** -- Dealer.com, Dealer Inspire, DealerOn. Where VDP forms and chat leads originate.

6. **Phone System / Call Tracking** -- CallRail, CallSource, Car Wars. Attribution for which leads come from which source.

7. **Texting / Messaging Platform** -- Podium, Kenect. If the dealer already has one, your AI needs to use their existing channel, not create a new one.

### Nice-to-Have (Differentiation)

8. **Service Scheduling** -- Xtime, Tekion Service. Cross-selling service appointments from sales AI.
9. **F&I / Desking Tools** -- Integration enables AI to provide payment estimates.
10. **Google Business Profile / Reviews** -- Reputation management tie-in.
11. **Facebook / Meta Lead Ads** -- Direct ingestion of social media leads.

### Key Integration Insight

The CADA study found that **55% of Canadian dealers cite integration challenges** as a top pain point, and dealers often operate **20+ overlapping systems** without a unified strategy. Any solution that reduces integration friction has a massive competitive advantage.

| **Mid-Market** | $500-$1,200/mo | AI BDC (voice + text + email), CRM integration, lead qualification | Single-rooftop franchised dealers |
| **Enterprise** | $1,500-$3,000+/mo | Full lifecycle AI, multi-channel, analytics dashboard, dedicated support | Dealer groups, multi-rooftop |
| **Custom/Platform** | $3,000-$10,000+/mo | White-label, API access, custom integrations, OEM program tie-ins | Large dealer groups, OEM programs |

### Common Pricing Structures

1. **Flat monthly fee per rooftop** -- Most common. One price per dealership location.
2. **Per-lead or per-conversation** -- Some AI chatbot vendors charge based on volume. Risky for dealers with unpredictable lead flow.
3. **Tiered by features** -- Base platform + add-ons (voice AI, inventory sync, analytics).
4. **Per-seat** -- Legacy CRM model. Being displaced by unlimited-user models (AutoRaptor charges $500-$1,500/mo for unlimited users).
5. **Revenue share / performance-based** -- Rare but emerging. Some vendors tie fees to appointments set or deals closed.

### Pricing Insight for Our Agency

The sweet spot for a Nexus-powered dealership automation offering appears to be:
- **Build fee:** $15,000-$35,000 (one-time setup, custom workflow development, integrations)
- **Monthly retainer:** $2,000-$5,000/mo (monitoring, optimization, n8n hosting, AI costs)
- This undercuts the enterprise AI BDC vendors while delivering more customization
- The transparency/ownership angle justifies the build fee ("you own this, it's not rented SaaS")

- **Activix CRM** is the dominant Canadian-focused CRM, trusted by **840+ dealerships** with a **97% customer retention rate**

- **PBS Systems** is a major Canadian DMS provider (headquartered in Calgary)

### Key Canadian Pain Points

- **"Dealers are not short of tools; they are short of alignment, integration, and consistent execution."**
- Dealers often operate **20+ overlapping systems** without a unified strategy
- Core systems (DMS, CRM) are widely adopted but score **below the industry satisfaction average**
- AI use cases are concentrated in **marketing and sales** (automated comms, vehicle descriptions) -- service and operations are underserved
- The largest group of decision-makers are **cautious followers** who want early adopters to prove the path first

### Canadian Regulatory Considerations

- **CASL (Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation):** Strict consent requirements for electronic messages. Any AI sending emails or texts must have documented express or implied consent. Penalties up to $10M per violation.
- **PIPEDA (Personal Information Protection):** Federal privacy law governing how dealerships collect, use, and share customer data. AI systems processing customer data must comply.
- **Provincial Privacy Laws:** Quebec (Law 25), Alberta, and BC have their own privacy legislation that may impose additional requirements.
- **AMVIC / OMVIC / Provincial Regulators:** Each province has a motor vehicle industry council with specific advertising and sales practice rules.

- Matador AI specifically markets its [Activix integration](https://matador.ai/activix-integration/) as a differentiator
- Building n8n workflows that connect to Activix CRM would immediately address the largest Canadian CRM installed base
- AutoSync (Activix parent) also operates AutoTrader.ca, Canada's largest vehicle marketplace, creating potential for inventory-to-lead-to-followup automation loops

## Strategic Recommendations

### 1. Position Against the Gap, Not the Leaders

Do not compete head-to-head with Podium or Impel on features. Instead, target the **integration and alignment gap** that the CADA study identified. Dealers have 20+ tools and none of them talk to each other. n8n-powered automation is the connective tissue.

### 2. Lead with the "47-Hour Problem"

The average dealership takes 47 hours to respond. AI gets it under 90 seconds. This is the single most compelling stat for any sales conversation. Pair it with "78% buy from the first responder."

### 3. Target Canadian Dealers First

- Activix has 840+ dealers with a CRM that has an API
- No major AI BDC vendor is Canadian-focused
- CASL compliance is a barrier that US vendors handle poorly
- The CADA study shows 60% have "some AI" but most of it is shallow (writing vehicle descriptions)
- The cautious-follower majority (largest segment) needs a trusted guide, not a SaaS product

### 4. Build the Cold Lead Reactivation Workflow as the Entry Point

This is the lowest-risk, highest-ROI first engagement:
- Every dealer has thousands of dead leads sitting in their CRM
- No one is following up on them systematically
- An n8n workflow that pulls cold leads from Activix, runs them through AI qualification, and sends personalized multi-channel sequences is a $15K build + $3K/mo retainer
- Results are measurable within 30 days (appointments booked from "dead" leads)

### 5. Price for the Mid-Market Sweet Spot

- Build: $15,000-$35,000 (vs. $0 for SaaS signup, but you own it)
- Monthly: $2,000-$5,000 (vs. $500-$1,500 for Numa/Podium, but fully customized)
- Justify the premium with: ownership, customization, CASL compliance, Activix-native integration, and the fact that you are local and Canadian
