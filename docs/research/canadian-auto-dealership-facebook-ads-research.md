## 1. TOP-PERFORMING AD FORMATS

**Ranked by performance (best to worst for auto dealers):**

### Tier 1: Highest Performers

**A. Facebook Lead Form Ads**
- Lead forms outperform landing pages with 50% lower cost per lead and 200% increase in lead volume (Source: Driftrock/MG Motor case study)
- Key: Include a qualifying question like "When do you intend to purchase?" -- leads buying within 3 months convert at the highest rates
- Users never leave Facebook, reducing friction dramatically
- Lead data syncs directly to CRM within seconds

**B. Dynamic Inventory / Automotive Inventory Ads (AIA)**
- Facebook Vehicle Listing Ads automatically sync dealership inventory feeds
- Creates dynamic, VIN-specific ads with real-time pricing and imagery
- 3.4x higher conversion rates than static ads (Source: Spyne.ai)
- Porsche Singapore used this format with integrated lead forms to showcase full catalogue

**C. Carousel Ads (Dynamic)**
- Real-time inventory integration from dealership websites
- Long-form ad copy with finance offers and local differentiators increases CTR considerably
- Produces leads at a fraction of the cost of 3rd party leads or Google PPC

### Tier 2: Strong Performers

**D. Video Ads (Short-Form Vertical)**
- 92% of social media video is watched without sound -- text overlays are mandatory
- Volkswagen Christmas stop-motion format performed well
- 15 seconds or less is the sweet spot
- Vertical 9:16 for Reels/Stories, 4:5 for Feed

**E. Click-to-Messenger / Chatbot Ads**
- Car Loans Canada case study: 44% reduction in CPA ($45 to $25 per applicant)
- 75% increase in conversion rate (from 5-8% to 25%)
- Chatbot pre-qualifies leads inside Messenger before sending to CRM
- Shorter applications via Facebook user data auto-fill

**F. Collection Ads**
- Dynamic real-time inventory based on browsing behavior
- Combines images, video, and CTAs in immersive mobile format
- Low cost-per-lead and low customer acquisition cost

### Tier 3: Supporting Role

**G. Single Image Ads**
- Best for brand awareness, service promotions, trade-in offers
- Use for retargeting with dealership differentiators (warranty, delivery, etc.)
- Simpler to produce but lower engagement than video/carousel

## 4. META AUDIENCE TARGETING FOR AUTO DEALERS

### CRITICAL: Special Ad Category Restrictions for Credit/Financing Ads

When running auto FINANCING ads (not just inventory ads), you MUST select "Credit" as a Special Ad Category. This restricts:

| Targeting Feature | Available? | Workaround |
|---|---|---|
| Age targeting | NO -- must be 18-65+ | Use ad creative to self-select |
| Gender targeting | NO -- must include all | Use imagery/copy to attract target |
| ZIP/postal code | NO -- 15-mile minimum radius | Target city + radius |
| Detailed interests | LIMITED -- many removed | Use broader categories |
| Lookalike audiences | NO -- not available | Use Special Ad Audiences instead |
| Custom audiences (CRM) | YES | Upload customer lists |
| Location (city/province) | YES | With 15-mile buffer |
| Retargeting (website visitors) | YES | Install Meta Pixel |

**Source:** jonloomer.com, data-axle.com, idimension.com, marketingnewscanada.com

### What DOES Work for Targeting:

**1. CRM-Based Custom Audiences**
- Upload past purchaser lists
- Target "past Ford truck purchasers" with new Ford truck inventory
- Exclude recent buyers using Meta offline events

**2. Website Retargeting**
- Pixel-based audiences from inventory page visitors
- Dynamic retargeting showing specific vehicles they viewed
- "Abandoned application" retargeting for finance leads

**3. Interest Categories Still Available:**
- Automobile industry (broad)
- Four-wheel drive vehicles
- Trucks
- Specific vehicle makes/models (though being consolidated)
- Agriculture and construction (industry interests)

**4. Behavioral Targeting:**
- "Likely to move" (life event)
- New job (life event)
- Recently engaged/married
- New parents
- Homeowners vs. renters

**5. Broad Targeting with Advantage+ (Meta's recommendation as of 2025-2026):**
- Meta has been consolidating detailed interests into broader categories since June 2025
- Many specific interests merged or removed
- Meta now recommends broader audiences + letting their algorithm optimize
- Source: faniq.live, jonloomer.com

## 5. TARGETING UNION WORKERS / HEALTHCARE / TRADES

### Available Meta Interest & Job Title Categories:

**IMPORTANT:** As of 2025-2026, Meta has significantly reduced detailed targeting. However, in the Ads Manager "Detailed Targeting" search box, you can still type and discover available options. The platform dynamically suggests what is available.

### Trades / Construction:
- **Interest categories:** Construction, Home improvement, Carpentry, Welding, Plumbing, Electrical work, HVAC
- **Industry interests:** Agriculture and construction workers (confirmed available)
- **Employer targeting:** Type specific employer names (e.g., "PCL Construction", "EllisDon")
- **Job title targeting:** Enter titles like "Electrician", "Plumber", "Welder", "Heavy Equipment Operator" -- Facebook shows matches if available
- **Behavioral:** Four-wheel drive interest, truck interest (proxy for trades demographics)

### Healthcare:
- **Job titles to search:** Registered Nurse, Nurse, Personal Support Worker, Paramedic, Healthcare Worker, Medical Professional
- **Interest categories:** Nursing, Healthcare, Medical field
- **Employer targeting:** Target specific hospital/health authority names (e.g., "Sunnybrook Hospital", "Alberta Health Services")
- **Professional associations:** Search for nursing associations, healthcare unions

### Union Workers:
- **NOTE:** Meta restricts ads related to trade unions as a "sensitive" category
- **Workaround:** Target by EMPLOYER (union shops) rather than union membership
- **Target employers like:** Unifor, CUPE, OPSEU, IBEW members' employers
- **Target by industry interest + location near major union employers (auto plants, hospitals, municipal works)

### Recommended Custom Audience Strategy:
Since Special Ad Category limits detailed targeting, the best approach is:
1. Build a customer list of past union/trades/healthcare buyers
2. Create a Custom Audience from that list
3. Let Meta's Advantage+ find similar people (replaces Lookalike)
4. Layer with geographic targeting near major employers in those sectors

**Source:** quora.com (job title targeting), searchengineland.com (medical targeting), aimclear.com (job title workarounds), facebook.com/business/help (detailed targeting)

## 6. ETHICAL ADS FOR 575-699 CREDIT RANGE

### The Compliance Landscape:

**What you CANNOT do on Meta:**
- Reference the user's credit score directly ("Bad credit? No problem!")
- Use "guaranteed approval" language (it is misleading and can get ads rejected)
- Target by credit score (not a Meta targeting option)
- Exclude or include by financial status under Special Ad Category

**What you CAN do (ethical approach):**

### Compliant Ad Copy Examples:

**Example A: Service-Focused (Recommended)**
- Headline: "Financing Options for Every Situation"
- Primary Text: "At [Dealership], we work with 20+ lenders to find the right fit for your budget and situation. Whether you're building credit, rebuilding, or starting fresh -- our finance specialists are here to help. No judgment. Just solutions. Apply online in 60 seconds."
- CTA: "Get Pre-Approved"
- WHY IT WORKS: Highlights the SERVICE (working with multiple lenders) rather than the PERSON'S situation

**Example B: Fresh Start Positioning**
- Headline: "A New Chapter Starts Here"
- Primary Text: "We believe everyone deserves reliable transportation. Our team specializes in finding financing solutions that work -- regardless of your credit history. Transparent terms. No hidden fees. Real people helping real people."
- CTA: "Learn More"
- WHY IT WORKS: Empathetic without being predatory. Does not promise specific outcomes.

**Example C: Payment-Focused (Avoids Credit Discussion)**
- Headline: "Quality Vehicles Starting at $199/month"
- Primary Text: "Looking for a reliable vehicle with payments that fit your budget? Browse our inventory of inspected, certified pre-owned vehicles. Multiple financing options available. Apply online -- get an answer in minutes, not days."
- CTA: "Shop Now"
- WHY IT WORKS: Focuses on affordability (payment), not credit worthiness

**Key Compliance Rules:**
- Facebook requires: "Instead of referencing the user directly ('Need a good car loan, but don't have great credit?'), highlight the store's service instead ('We offer car loans for all credit situations')"
- Must declare Special Ad Category: Credit
- All financing terms subject to truth-in-advertising laws
- In Canada: CASL compliance required for follow-up communications

**Source:** 9clouds.com (Special Ad Category), netsourcemedia.com (ad changes), facebook.com/business (policy)

|---|---|---|---|

| **February** | Valentine's/emotion | "Fall in Love With Your Next Vehicle" | LOW-MED |

| **April** | Spring clearance | "Spring Into Savings" | MEDIUM |
| **May** | End-of-school push | "Spring clearance -- Model Closeouts" | MEDIUM |
| **June** | Graduation | "First-Time Buyer? We Make It Easy" | MEDIUM-HIGH |

### Key Timing Insights:
- **Days 20-28 of any month:** Capture month-end urgency and manufacturer incentive deadlines
- **Tax refund season (Feb-April):** Highest opportunity for used vehicles and sub-prime financing
- **Model year clearance (Aug-Oct):** Best for new inventory at reduced pricing
- **Q4 (Nov-Dec):** Highest overall dealership traffic of the year
- **Plan campaigns ONE QUARTER in advance** for best results

### Canada-Specific Seasonal Factors:
- **Winter tire season (Oct-Nov):** Bundle with vehicle purchase ("Free winter tires with any vehicle purchase")
- **Spring thaw (March-April):** Pent-up demand from winter -- people want to shop after being stuck inside
- **Summer road trip (June-July):** Family vehicle upgrades for vacation travel
- **Back to school (August):** Parents buying for university students

**Source:** actualseomedia.com, bedfordadvertising.com, willowoodventures.com

## 9. VIDEO AD HOOKS -- FIRST 3 SECONDS

### The Data:
- Average scroll speed increased 41% since 2020
- You have 0.5 seconds to capture attention before they scroll past
- Average mobile viewing duration: 1.7 seconds
- Hook Rate benchmark: Average 15-20%, Good 20-40%, Excellent 40%+
- 92% of video watched on mute -- design for mute-first

### The 4-Part Hook Structure (all in first 3 seconds):
1. **Text overlay** (the words on screen)
2. **Sound hook** (audio for those with sound on)
3. **Visual hook** (motion/image that stops the scroll)
4. **Vibe** (emotional tone/energy)

### Specific Video Hook Scripts for Auto Dealers:

**Hook Type 1: Problem-Solution**
- Text on screen: "Tired of getting turned down?"
- Visual: Person looking frustrated, then smiling
- Voice: "What if I told you there's a better way?"
- Automotive version: "Tired of unreliable rides? We have the answer."

**Hook Type 2: Statistic/Proof**
- Text on screen: "98% of our customers get approved"
- Visual: Quick montage of happy buyers driving away
- Voice: "Here's why hundreds of Canadians trust [Dealer]..."
- Automotive version: "83% of car buyers research online before visiting. Here's what they find..."

**Hook Type 3: Challenge/Reverse Psychology**
- Text on screen: "DON'T watch this if you like overpaying"
- Visual: Someone signing paperwork, looking shocked at price
- Voice: "Only click if you want a fair deal."
- Creates exclusivity and challenges the viewer

**Hook Type 4: FOMO/Urgency**
- Text on screen: "Only 3 left at this price"
- Visual: Lot with "SOLD" signs on vehicles
- Voice: "This deal ends Sunday. Don't miss it."

**Hook Type 5: Pattern Interrupt**
- Text on screen: "STOP. Your car payment is too high."
- Visual: Someone ripping up a bill
- Voice: "We just saved [Name] $200/month. You're next."

**Hook Type 6: Oddly Satisfying / ASMR**
- Text on screen: "Just watch."
- Visual: Slow-motion door close, leather interior detail, engine start
- No voice -- pure visual appeal
- Works for premium inventory

**Hook Type 7: POV / Walkthrough**
- Text on screen: "POV: You just got approved"
- Visual: First-person walk through lot, sit in car, drive away
- Voice: Ambient lot sounds, engine purr
- Native platform feel -- looks like content not an ad

**Hook Type 8: Myth Buster**
- Text on screen: "Most people negotiate WRONG"
- Visual: Dealer behind desk, quick cut to price dropping
- Voice: "Forget everything you thought you knew about buying a car."

### Production Tips:
- Shoot VERTICAL (9:16) for Reels/Stories, 4:5 for Feed
- Under 15 seconds total
- Real cars and real people beat stock footage
- Assume mute -- every point must be readable as text
- Cut every 2-3 seconds to maintain attention

**Source:** metalla.digital, segwise.ai, motionapp.com, dealersunited.com, influee.co

## KEY RECOMMENDATIONS

1. **Start with Lead Form Ads + Dynamic Inventory Ads** -- these are the two highest-converting formats with the lowest cost per lead for auto dealers.

2. **For sub-prime/financing ads, always declare Special Ad Category: Credit** -- or risk ad account suspension. Work within the restrictions using CRM audiences and broad targeting.

3. **Use the Car Loans Canada chatbot model** -- Messenger-based chatbot pre-qualification cut CPA by 44% and increased conversions by 75%. This is the single most impactful tactic found in this research.

4. **For Quebec, all ads must be French-first** -- and the "2eme et 3eme chance" positioning is standard market language. Adapt templates above.

5. **Focus video hooks on the first 0.5 seconds** -- text overlay + pattern interrupt visual. Assume mute viewing. Keep videos under 15 seconds.

6. **For targeting trades/healthcare/union workers:** Use employer-based targeting (major hospitals, construction firms, municipal employers) rather than trying to target by union membership or job title, which has been increasingly restricted.

7. **Peak seasons for ad spend:** March (tax refunds), September (model year clearance), November-December (Black Friday + year-end). Scale budget UP during these windows.

8. **Month-end urgency (days 20-28) drives conversions** -- align event campaigns with manufacturer incentive deadlines.

9. **Avoid "guaranteed approval" language** -- it is both non-compliant with Meta policies and potentially deceptive. Use "financing for every situation" or "we work with 20+ lenders" positioning instead.

10. **Clutch's insight is worth noting:** In categories where trust is critical, Meta ads alone are insufficient. The 70/30 performance-to-brand ratio is worth considering for established dealers.
