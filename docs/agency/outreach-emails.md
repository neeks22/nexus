# Outreach Email Templates

These templates are starting points, not scripts. Personalize every email before sending — especially the first line. If it reads like a template, it gets deleted.

**Ground rules:**
- Always research the company for 5 minutes before sending. Know one specific thing about them.
- Send from your personal email, not a mass-send tool. These are conversations, not campaigns.
- Subject lines are everything. Test them. The ones below work — don't get creative until you have data.
- Never attach anything to a cold email. Links only.
- Follow-up is where deals are made. Most replies come on the third touch.

---

## Email 1: Cold Outreach

**Timing:** Initial contact
**Subject options (A/B test these):**
- `[Company]'s AI agents — quick question`
- `Why 88% of AI agent projects fail`
- `Noticed something about [Company]'s tech stack`

---

Hi [First Name],

I noticed [Company] is using [specific AI tool or agent system — e.g., a customer-facing chatbot / automated lead routing / AI document processing]. Most companies at your stage are running into the same problem: the agents work in demos, but they're fragile in production.

The number that stops people: **88% of AI agent projects fail before reaching production.** Not because the AI is bad — because there's no infrastructure to keep the agents running when things go wrong. One rate limit. One bad API response. One hallucination nobody catches. The whole pipeline dies.

We built Nexus specifically for this. Every agent we build has self-healing built in — circuit breakers so one failure doesn't cascade, automatic recovery so the system keeps running at 2am without waking anyone up, and health scoring so you can see problems before they become incidents.

The result: our clients see 40% fewer production incidents and recover 89% of failures automatically.

I have a specific idea for how this could apply to [Company]'s [specific process]. Worth a 20-minute call?

[Your name]
Nexus AI Agency
[Calendar link]

---

*Personalization notes:*
- *[Company]'s AI tool or agent system* — find this on their website, job postings, or recent LinkedIn posts. If they're posting "AI Engineer" jobs, they're building. If their website mentions a chatbot, they're running agents. Specific is everything.
- *[specific process]* — pick one: customer support, lead qualification, document processing. Don't say "your business."
- *Keep it under 150 words.* This template is already near the limit. Cut, don't add.

## Email 2: Follow-Up #1

**Timing:** 3 days after Email 1 (no reply)
**Subject:** `Re: [Company]'s AI agents — quick question` *(reply to the same thread)*

---

Hi [First Name],

Following up on my note from [day — e.g., Monday].

Quick specific question: when your [chatbot / lead routing system / automation] fails today, what happens? Does it degrade gracefully, or does someone have to manually restart it?

I ask because that single question usually reveals whether a team has self-healing infrastructure or not. If the answer is "someone has to restart it," you're probably spending 3-5 hours/month on maintenance you shouldn't be doing.

Happy to show you what the alternative looks like — no pitch, just a look at how the self-healing layer works. 15 minutes on [day or two options].

[Your name]

---

*Notes: This email exists to get a reply, even a negative one. "We handle it ourselves" is a great reply — it opens a conversation. "What do you mean by self-healing?" is even better. The goal is engagement, not a yes.*

## Email 5: Proposal Send

**Timing:** When sending the proposal document
**Subject:** `[Company] × Nexus — AI Agent Proposal`

---

Hi [First Name],

Attached is the proposal we discussed. Here's what's in it and why I built it this way:

**What we're solving:**
[1-2 sentences: the specific problem — their actual pain point in their language, not ours]

**What we're building:**
[X] AI agents: [quick description — e.g., "an intake agent that monitors your inbox, a qualification agent that scores leads against your criteria, and a recovery agent that handles failures automatically"]. Built on Nexus's self-healing framework — circuit breakers and automatic recovery built in.

**The numbers:**
- Estimated annual savings: $[X]
- Build investment: $[X] (Option A) or $[X]/month (Option C)
- Payback period: [X] weeks

**Timeline:** 6 weeks from signed SOW to production. Milestone-based — you see progress every week, not just at the end.

**Our guarantee:** If it doesn't meet the acceptance criteria in the SOW, we keep working until it does. No charge.

The proposal is valid for 30 days. Happy to walk through it on a call if anything needs clarification — just grab time here: [calendar link]

Ready to move forward? Sign the proposal and I'll send the invoice for the deposit. We can start as early as [date].

[Your name]
[Phone]

---

*Notes:*
- *Attach the proposal as a PDF, not a link to an editable document.*
- *"Option A, B, C" in the proposal gives them something to choose rather than a binary yes/no. Most people pick Option B — it's the middle.*
- *The guarantee mention is important. Most agencies have no guarantee. Yours does. Say it.*
- *If they go quiet after the proposal, follow up once at Day 3, once at Day 7, once at Day 14. After that, monthly check-in until they say no.*

## General Sending Notes

**Cadence for a warm prospect:**
- Email 1 (cold outreach)
- Day 3: Email 2 (follow-up)
- Day 7: Email 3 (case study)
- Day 14: One last "is now a bad time?" — 2 sentences max
- After that: Monthly check-in for up to 6 months, then archive

**What "no" sounds like:** "Not a fit," "already have a solution," "no budget this year." Respect it and move on.

**What "not yet" sounds like:** No reply, "we're exploring," "maybe next quarter." These stay in monthly check-in rotation.

**Track everything in a simple spreadsheet:** Contact, company, email sent date, reply, outcome. You'll start to see patterns in what's working within 20 outreaches.
