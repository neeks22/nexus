# NESB Copywriting Framework
## Kyle Milligan's "Take Their Money" — The Big 4

> "Your prospect has 3 seconds to decide if they care. NESB is how you win those 3 seconds."

---

## Overview

NESB is a direct response copywriting framework extracted from Kyle Milligan's *Take Their Money*. It identifies the four core emotional triggers that compel people to take action. Every piece of copy you write — ads, emails, SMS, landing pages, scripts — should hit at least 3 of the 4.

**The framework is built on one insight:** people don't buy products. They buy feelings. NESB maps those feelings to power words and structures that bypass rational skepticism and speak directly to the emotional subtext driving the decision.

For dealership marketing specifically, NESB is a weapon. Your prospect has been burned before (bad credit, bad experience, predatory dealers). They're skeptical. They've heard every pitch. **Categorization is death** — the moment they file your ad under "another car dealer" it's over. NESB keeps them reading.

---

## The 4 Elements

---

### N — NEW (Alter Ego: ONLY)

**The emotional subtext:** *"If it's new, maybe THIS time it works for me."*

**The core insight:** Humans are wired to pay attention to novelty. New = hope. When something is new, it hasn't failed yet. It carries the possibility of being different from everything that came before. For subprime customers especially — who have been rejected, embarrassed, and talked down to — "new" means a new shot at something they've been denied.

**What ONLY does:** ONLY amplifies NEW by removing alternatives. If something is new AND only available here, the urgency compounds. Exclusivity signals value. It also creates FOMO — if only one place has it, there's a reason.

**The categorization trap:** The moment your prospect's brain says "this is just another car dealer ad," they stop reading. They've seen it. They know how it ends. NEVER let them categorize you. Lead with something they haven't filed away yet.

**Power Words:**
- Breakthrough
- Revolutionary
- Discovery
- Game-Changing
- Announcing
- Revealed
- Exclusive
- Patented
- Only here
- Proprietary
- First-ever
- Introducing

**ONLY amplifiers:**
- "Exclusively at [Dealership]"
- "The only Ottawa dealership that..."
- "Nowhere else in the region"
- "Our proprietary approval system"
- "You won't find this at any other lot"

**Rule:** New and *different* is always better than *better*. Don't say you're better. Say you're different. "Better" invites comparison. "Different" ends the comparison.

**Dealership Application:**
- New approval process, not a new car
- New program, not a new policy
- New path to ownership, not a new inventory
- The vehicle is secondary — lead with the new *opportunity*

---

### E — EASY (Alter Ego: ANYBODY)

**The emotional subtext:** *"I don't want to work for this. And if anybody can do it, even someone like me can."*

**The core insight:** There are two emotions at play with EASY. First, laziness — nobody wants effort. They want results without friction. Second, and more powerful: **insecurity**. For a subprime customer who has been rejected or made to feel lesser, "anybody" is deeply reassuring. It means *you don't have to be special to qualify*. You don't have to have perfect credit. You don't have to earn above a threshold. Anybody. Even you.

**The ANYBODY amplifier:** "Anybody can" removes the perceived barrier to entry. It's the opposite of the velvet rope. It says: we built this for people like you, not despite people like you.

**Not Statements — The Objection Killer:**
This is one of the most powerful techniques in the framework. Before your prospect objects out loud, you kill the objection in copy. You name the thing they're afraid of — and then you remove it.

Structure: *"No X required. No Y needed. Not even Z matters."*

This does three things:
1. Shows you understand their fear
2. Removes the barrier before they raise it
3. Builds trust by being honest about what they're worried about

**Power Words:**
- Simple
- Push-button
- Automatic
- Step-by-step
- System
- "All you have to do is..."
- Done for you
- No experience required
- In minutes
- As easy as
- Effortless
- Hands-free

**Dealership Application:**
- The approval process is the product. Make it sound effortless.
- "All you have to do is answer 5 questions"
- "Our system does the matching — you just pick your car"
- Not Statements: "No perfect credit. No large down payment. Not even a co-signer."

---

### S — SAFE (Alter Ego: PREDICTABLE)

**The emotional subtext:** *"I've been burned before. Show me this isn't a trap."*

**The core insight:** Humans are loss-averse. Research shows people will choose a guaranteed $80 over an 80% chance at $100. The certain outcome, even if smaller, beats the better-odds gamble. This is your prospect's brain every time they see a car ad — especially subprime customers who have been deceived by "zero down" offers that turned into $3,000 in fees.

**What PREDICTABLE does:** PREDICTABLE reduces skepticism by pointing to patterns. If it happened before, it can happen again. If others like me got approved, I can get approved. If there's a track record, there's predictability. Skepticism lives in uncertainty. Remove the uncertainty.

**The three tools of SAFE:**
1. **Testimonials** — Real people like them who succeeded. Named, specific, with details.
2. **Track records** — Numbers that show history. "847 families approved last year."
3. **Guarantees** — Absorb the risk. Put it on paper.

**Power Words:**
- Proven
- Guaranteed
- Track record
- Happened before
- History repeating
- Verified
- Certified
- 93% approval rate
- [X] families helped
- [X] years in business
- No catch
- Our promise

**Dealership Application:**
- Lead with social proof before the offer
- "Over 400 Ottawa families approved this year — even with past bankruptcies"
- "Same-day approvals since 2019"
- Published testimonials with real names and specific outcomes
- Money-back guarantees where applicable (documentation fee, etc.)
- Compliance angle: transparency builds SAFE faster than any claim

---

### B — BIG (Alter Ego: FAST)

**The emotional subtext:** *"This is huge. And if I don't move now, I'll miss it."*

**The core insight:** BIG demands attention. Small things don't stop the scroll. But FAST is what converts attention into action. BIG without FAST is interesting. BIG with FAST is urgent. The combination creates FOMO — the sense that something massive is available right now, but won't be tomorrow.

**What FAST does:** FAST multiplies BIG. "A life-changing opportunity" is big. "A life-changing opportunity that expires Friday" is a reason to act today. Time compression forces decisions. Every hour of delay is a potential loss.

**The attention equation:** Your headline must be so big and so fast that stopping feels like the wrong choice. Don't lead with the car. Lead with the outcome. "Drive home in a new car today" is bigger and faster than "Browse our inventory."

**Power Words:**
- Monster
- Massive
- Huge
- Once in a lifetime
- Overnight
- Instantly
- Today
- This week only
- Before [date]
- Limited spots
- [X] vehicles remaining
- Immediate approval
- Same-day
- Drive home today

**Dealership Application:**
- Always attach a deadline, even a soft one
- Inventory scarcity is real — use it
- "3 vehicles left at this price"
- "Weekend sale ends Sunday"
- "10 spots in our approval pipeline this week"
- Drive-home-today is the ultimate FAST close

## The 4 Sections of Direct Response Copy

Every piece of long-form copy follows this structure. Internalize it.

### 1. Headline
**Job:** Stop the scroll. Force the first read.

The headline must contain at least N or B (ideally both). It should be specific, promise a benefit, and never be cute at the expense of clarity. If they don't read the headline, nothing else matters.

*Formula options:*
- NEW + BIG: "Breakthrough Approval Program Puts Ottawa Families in New Cars — Even With Bad Credit"
- BIG + FAST: "847 Approvals in 2025 — Drive Home Today"
- NEW + SAFE: "The Only Ottawa Dealer With a Same-Day No-Risk Approval Guarantee"

### 2. Lead
**Job:** Reward them for reading past the headline. Deepen the hook.

The lead (first 3-5 sentences) must:
- Validate their pain or fear
- Promise the big outcome
- Signal this is different from everything they've seen before

Never start the lead with "We." Start with them.

*Formula:* Pain acknowledgment → New frame → Big promise

### 3. Body
**Job:** Build belief. Stack SAFE. Pre-handle objections with Not Statements.

This is where EASY and SAFE do the most work. Walk them through the process step by step. Kill objections before they form. Stack testimonials and social proof. Show the track record.

*Structure:*
- What it is (in plain language)
- How it works (step by step — EASY)
- Who it's for (ANYBODY)
- Proof it works (SAFE)
- Not Statements (kill top 3 objections)

### 4. Offer
**Job:** Make the decision obvious. Add urgency.

The offer section contains your BIG/FAST close. This is where you:
- Restate the value
- Name the action (one clear CTA)
- Apply scarcity/urgency
- Remove remaining risk (guarantee)

Never have two CTAs. One action. One button. One phone number.

## Not Statements — Deep Dive

Not Statements are the single most underused technique in dealership copy. Here's how to master them.

**The principle:** Your prospect has objections they will never say out loud. They'll just leave. Not Statements make you the one who names their fear — which instantly makes you different from every dealer who pretended the fear doesn't exist.

**Structure:**
```
[Benefit] — No [Objection 1] required.
Not even [Objection 2] matters here.
[Objection 3]? We've seen it all.
```

**Top objections for subprime dealership marketing:**
1. "My credit is too bad"
2. "I don't have a down payment"
3. "I've been declined everywhere"
4. "I need a co-signer"
5. "My income isn't stable enough"
6. "I have a repossession on my record"
7. "I'm in a consumer proposal / bankruptcy"
8. "I haven't been employed long enough"

**Example Not Statement block:**
> Get approved today. No perfect credit needed. No $5,000 down. Not even a co-signer. Had a repossession? We work with it. Consumer proposal? Approved daily. All you need is a job and the desire to own a vehicle.

**Why this works:** It reads like a checklist of reasons NOT to apply — and then removes every one of them. The reader thinks "wait, but what about my..." and before they finish the thought, you've already answered it.

## Applying NESB to Dealership Marketing

### Subprime Marketing (575-649 Credit)

**Primary emotional lever:** SAFE + NEW

The subprime customer is emotionally exhausted from rejection. They've built walls. Your job is to show them a new door they haven't tried yet, and that it's safe to walk through it.

- Lead with SAFE: "Over 400 Ottawa families helped this year"
- Introduce NEW: "Our approval system works differently than any bank or dealer"
- Use ANYBODY: "Even if you've been declined 10 times"
- Close with FAST: "Get a decision in 20 minutes"

**Tone:** Warm, non-judgmental, matter-of-fact. Never condescending. Never desperate.

### Near-Prime Marketing (650-699 Credit)

**Primary emotional lever:** NEW + EASY

This customer is close to standard rates. They're frustrated that "close" isn't "there yet." Show them a new path — a program designed exactly for where they are.

- Lead with NEW: "A financing tier most Ottawa dealers never show you"
- Use EASY: "One application, multiple lender options"
- Use SAFE: "Rates starting at [X]% for your credit tier"
- Close with BIG: "You may qualify for more than you think"

### Service Department Marketing

**Primary emotional lever:** SAFE + EASY

Service customers don't want surprises. They want predictable, simple, trustworthy.

- SAFE: "Certified techs. Manufacturer-approved parts. Written quotes before any work."
- EASY: "Drop off, we do everything, pick up when it's done"
- NEW: "Now offering Saturday drop-off with same-week turnaround"
- FAST: "Most jobs done same day"

### Retargeting (Warm Audiences)

**Primary emotional lever:** BIG + FAST

They already know you. They haven't acted. Give them a reason to move now.

- BIG: Remind them of the outcome they wanted
- FAST: Add urgency they didn't have before
- SAFE: Add social proof they didn't see before
- Kill the objection that stopped them

## NESB Scoring System

Score every piece of copy before publishing. Aim for 12+ out of 20.

| Element | Score (1–5) | What 5 Looks Like |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| **NEW** | /5 | Genuinely novel frame, never-seen-before angle, strong ONLY element |
| **EASY** | /5 | Clear Not Statements, step-by-step process shown, ANYBODY language throughout |
| **SAFE** | /5 | Specific numbers, named testimonials, explicit guarantee, track record stated |
| **BIG** | /5 | Massive outcome promised, hard deadline or scarcity, FAST close with one CTA |
| **Total** | /20 | |

**Scoring guide:**
- 16–20: Fire. Ship it.
- 12–15: Strong. Sharpen the lowest element.
- 8–11: Needs work. What emotion is missing?
- Below 8: Rewrite from scratch using the formula.

**Quick audit questions:**
- N: What's new or exclusive about this? Could they see this anywhere else?
- E: Does this sound like work? Are there any objections I haven't killed?
- S: Why should they trust this? Where's the proof?
- B: Is the outcome big enough? Is there a reason to act today and not next week?

**Before (weak):**
