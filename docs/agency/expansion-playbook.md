## 90-Day Check-In

### Email Template

**Subject:** 90-day check-in — how are the agents holding up?

---

Hi [First Name],

It's been 90 days since we launched your Nexus build. I wanted to reach out personally to see how things are running and make sure you're getting full value from what we built.

A few things I'd love to cover on a quick call:

- What's working well (and what you wish worked differently)
- Any new workflows or teams that have come up since we wrapped
- Whether the agents are being used the way we intended — or whether they've evolved

Would 20 minutes this week work? Here's my calendar: [Calendly link]

Looking forward to catching up.

— [Founder Name]

---

### 90-Day Check-In Call Agenda (20–30 min)

1. **Quick wins recap** (5 min) — What's working? What are they proud of?
2. **Pain points** (5 min) — What's still manual? What's broken or clunky?
3. **Usage check** (5 min) — Are the agents actually being used? Which ones? By who?
4. **Scope creep signals** (5 min) — Have other teams asked about it? New use cases mentioned?
5. **Next steps** (5 min) — Either: "great, let's revisit at 6 months" or "let's talk about Phase 2"

## Upsell Triggers

These are the signals that tell you a client is ready to expand. You don't need all of them — one strong signal is enough to open the conversation.

### Signal 1: "Can we do this for department X too?"
The most obvious one. When a client proactively asks if the system can be extended, they've already sold themselves. Respond: "Yes — and here's what that would look like." Don't wait for a formal proposal request.

### Signal 2: Consistently high agent utilization
If your monthly reports show agents running at 80%+ capacity or query volume growing month over month, the system is proving its value. This is data for a natural "we're outgrowing the current setup" conversation.

### Signal 3: New pain points surface in check-in calls
Clients often mention new problems casually — "we're still doing X manually" or "we wish the system could also handle Y." Every one of those is a scoped expansion opportunity. Log them. Bring them back with a solution at the next touchpoint.

### Signal 4: They refer you to someone else
A referral is the highest-trust signal in the relationship. A client who sends you new business is a client who is proud of what you built. Strike while the iron is hot — follow up on the referral, then circle back to the referring client: "We'd love to do for you what we're about to do for them."
