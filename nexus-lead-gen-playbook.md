## 1. FIRST PRINCIPLES

*These 8 laws govern every lead generation decision at Nexus. They are platform-agnostic, tool-agnostic, and timeless.*

### Principle 1: A Lead Is a Decaying Asset
A lead is not a data point. It is a moment of vulnerability — a human who encountered a gap between their current reality and their desired reality, and a vehicle sits in that gap. Every second between intent formation and resolution, value is lost. The entire system must be designed to collapse the time between "I want" and "I have."

### Principle 2: Arrive Before the Search
The highest-value intervention happens BEFORE the search, not during it. Intent is born from life events — a birth, a death, a job change, a breakdown, a breakup, a status shift. Whoever owns the pre-intent signal owns the lead before it becomes a lead. The game is not to bid higher on keywords. The game is to be present at Stage -3 where there is no competition, no ad auction, and no noise.

**Where intent lives before the search:**
- Stage -3 (days-weeks before): HR platforms, real estate listings, parenting forums, insurance claims, mechanic quotes
- Stage -2 (hours-days before): YouTube lifestyle channels, Reddit r/PersonalFinanceCanada, TikTok life-upgrade content, credit score apps
- Stage -1 (minutes-hours before): Autotrader.ca, Google Maps dealer reviews, bank pre-approval tools
- Stage 0: The Google search — by this point, the buyer is 80% decided

### Principle 3: The Funnel Begins at the Life Event
Three massive leaks exist before any dealership system sees a name:
- 40% never get past internal negotiation ("Do I really need a new car?")
- 30% never make contact due to anxiety (fear of rejection, pressure, financial mistake)
- The remaining drop-off at first contact is the THIRD leak, not the first

### Principle 4: Misalignment Kills Leads, Not Neglect
The buyer wants a guide. The dealer deploys a hunter. The moment the buyer senses they are prey, trust collapses and the lead dies. It doesn't matter if follow-up happens in 30 seconds or 30 minutes — if the nature of the follow-up is adversarial, the lead is dead.

### Principle 5: The First Trusted Voice Wins
Be early. Be helpful. Be honest. The order matters. "Congratulations on [life event]. When you're ready, here's what your options look like — no commitment, no pressure, just clarity."

### Principle 6: Eliminate Follow-Up Entirely
The perfect system anticipates needs so there is nothing to follow up on. Every question is pre-answered. Every friction point is pre-removed.

### Principle 7: Structural Trust Is the Unfair Advantage
AI can be genuinely, structurally, unconditionally on the buyer's side in a way that no commissioned salesperson ever can. AI has no commission, no quota, no ego, no fear of rejection, no bad days, no favoritism. When the buyer genuinely trusts the system, conversion rates don't incrementally improve — they step-change.

### Principle 8: Serve Urgency, Never Exploit It
The subprime buyer who is treated with dignity becomes your most valuable long-term asset. They don't forget who treated them with respect when no one else would.

**Resolution:** Radical financial transparency before emotional investment. Acknowledge the buyer's history. Give them control. Small commitments, not big leaps. Post-approval care with a credit rebuilding plan.

### Testing Framework

**Week 1:** Test 3 Instant Form hooks per ad set (test the OFFER, not the creative). 2 creative formats (static vs 15-sec video). Do NOT test audiences, bid strategies, or placements yet.

**Week 2-3:** Minimum samples: 1,000 impressions AND 10 clicks per ad, 5+ conversions per variant. Sort by CPA. Check lead quality via show rate, not just volume.

**Week 4 Kill/Scale Rules:**
- Kill if: CPA >$65 after $100+ spend, CTR <0.5% (Meta) / <2.0% (Google), lead-to-show <15%
- Scale if: CPA <$30 for 5+ days (increase 20% every 3 days), lead-to-show >40%
- Refresh if: Frequency >3.5, CTR drops 30% from peak, ad running 21+ days

## 8. DEBATE RESOLUTIONS

Six challenges were argued. Six adjustments were made:
