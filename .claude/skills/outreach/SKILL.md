# Outreach Process

## When to Use
When sending cold outreach to potential clients.

## CASL Compliance (REQUIRED for Canadian Recipients)
Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation requires:
1. **Sender identification**: Your full name, company name, mailing address
2. **Unsubscribe mechanism**: "Reply STOP to unsubscribe" or unsubscribe link
3. **Consent basis**: You must have one of:
   - Express consent (they opted in)
   - Implied consent via business relationship (they're a customer)
   - Implied consent via conspicuous publication (their email is on their website for business purposes)
4. **Record keeping**: Log the consent basis for each contact
5. **Business addresses only**: Never email personal addresses

Include this footer on every email:
```
Nexus AI Agency | [Your Address], Ottawa, ON
Reply STOP to unsubscribe from future messages.
```

## LinkedIn DM Template
"Hey [Name] — saw [something specific about their company].
Quick question: how is your team handling [specific pain point]?
We've been helping similar companies automate [X]% of that with AI agents.
Worth a 15-minute call? No pitch, just a quick demo of what's possible."

## Follow-up Cadence
| Day | Channel | Action |
|-----|---------|--------|
| 0 | Email | First touch (pain-focused) |
| 1 | LinkedIn | Connect request with note |
| 3 | Email | Follow-up (value add) |
| 5 | LinkedIn | Comment on their content |
| 7 | Email | Case study share |
| 10 | LinkedIn | DM with specific insight |
| 14 | Email | Urgency / limited availability |
| 21 | Email | Breakup (last chance) |

## Research Before Outreach
Use the client-researcher agent to gather:
- Company overview, size, decision makers
- Current pain points (from job postings, reviews, news)
- Specific process to reference in the email

## Tracking
Log every outreach in a spreadsheet or CRM:
- Contact name, email, company
- Consent basis (published email / business relationship)
- Date of each touch
- Response received (Y/N)
- Status (new / contacted / replied / meeting booked / proposal sent / closed)
